ARR / EMNLP 2026 practical submission workflow for INSIGHT

Goal:
Submit the current paper first to ARR, selecting EMNLP 2026 as the preferred venue, and prepare the package so it can later be committed to EMNLP after reviews/meta-review.

Working files in this package:
- Full PDF: insight_emnlp.pdf
- First-25-page PDF: insight_emnlp_first25.pdf
- Main source entrypoint: insight_emnlp.tex
- Submission notes/checklist: submission_package_notes.txt
- Last-minute upload check: ARR_LAST_MINUTE_CHECKLIST.md

Recommended workflow:

Phase 1: Freeze the scientific package before ARR submission
1. Read the latest full PDF manually from page 1 to the appendix start.
2. Confirm the title, abstract, and contribution framing are final enough for review.
3. Confirm no further last-minute experiment is required.
4. Freeze one exact version of:
   - insight_emnlp.tex and all sec_*_emnlp.tex files
   - insight.bib
   - compiled insight_emnlp.pdf
5. Save a timestamped backup copy of the frozen submission package.
   Current helper:
   - `paper/emnlp2026/freeze_arr_submission.sh`
   Default naming now uses:
   - `frozen/ARR<UTC timestamp>/`
   - `frozen/insight_emnlp_arr_freeze_ARR<UTC timestamp>.tar.gz`

Phase 2: Authorship and policy readiness
1. Finalize author list before ARR submission.
2. Make sure every coauthor knows:
   - ARR submission deadline
   - reviewer registration deadline
   - author-response discussion window
   - EMNLP commitment timeline
3. Confirm that all authors are prepared to complete required ARR reviewer registration.
4. Confirm no dual submission conflict exists.
5. Confirm no author-list changes will be needed later; EMNLP commitment does not allow adding/removing authors.

Phase 3: Final anonymity and compliance pass
1. Check the PDF for any lab, institution, or personal identifiers.
2. Check appendix text for hidden identity leaks in asset descriptions or release plans.
3. Check bibliography for self-citations that accidentally de-anonymize the paper.
4. Confirm limitations are explicitly stated.
5. Confirm no unsupported or hallucinated citation remains.
6. Confirm any AI/LLM assistance used in the method is accurately described.
   Current helper:
   - `paper/emnlp2026/run_submission_sanity_checks.sh`

Phase 4: ARR submission steps
1. Submit the anonymized paper to ARR.
2. Choose EMNLP 2026 as the preferred venue during ARR submission.
3. Upload the correct anonymized PDF.
4. If supplementary material is uploaded separately, ensure it is anonymized and consistent with the submitted PDF.
5. Record the ARR submission ID and exact upload timestamp.
6. Keep the exact submitted PDF and sources in a frozen archive.

Phase 5: Immediately after ARR submission
1. Verify that all authors complete required reviewer registration by the ARR deadline.
2. Create a review-response tracking document for later rebuttal.
   Current helper:
   - `EMNLP_REVIEW_RESPONSE_TRACKER.md`
3. Prepare a short evidence map for likely reviewer questions:
   - why language-grounded semantic interface?
   - why ontology construction is a modeling choice?
   - what is the strongest evidence vs. current limitations?
   - why actor-based timing is only partial evidence?
   Current working drafts:
   - `EMNLP_REVIEW_RESPONSE_MAP.md`
   - `EMNLP_INTERVENTION_STATUS.md`
   - `EMNLP_CONTROLLED_ONTOLOGY_STATUS.md`
4. Keep all post-submission edits in a new revision branch/folder; do not overwrite the frozen submitted version.

Phase 6: After ARR reviews arrive
1. Read reviews and group them into:
   - framing / positioning issues
   - evidence gaps
   - clarity / writing issues
   - protocol / robustness concerns
2. Draft author response with direct claim-evidence mapping.
3. Decide whether to:
   - commit current reviewed paper to EMNLP 2026, or
   - revise and resubmit to a later ARR cycle.

Phase 7: If committing to EMNLP 2026
1. Use the reviewed ARR paper for EMNLP commitment.
2. Keep the same author set; only author order may change if needed.
3. Align the final EMNLP commitment package with reviewer feedback.
4. If accepted, prepare the camera-ready version using the final ACL publication instructions and updated page allowance.

Practical advice specific to this project:
- Keep the paper positioned as a language-grounded multimodal semantics / ontology paper, not as a pure accident-prediction leaderboard paper.
- Keep the strongest claims on ontology-as-modeling, semantic auditability, and beyond-accuracy evaluation.
- Keep the current caution that actor-based timing evidence is partial and dataset-dependent.
- Do not reopen large experimental branches unless they clearly change the acceptance picture.

Suggested frozen package naming convention:
- `frozen/ARR20260416T135900Z/`
- `frozen/insight_emnlp_arr_freeze_ARR20260416T135900Z.tar.gz`
