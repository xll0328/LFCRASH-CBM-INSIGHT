# Threats to Validity and Scope Boundaries

## 1. Protocol separation
The project contains canonical submission lines, controlled ontology comparisons, support analyses, and search runs. These must not be mixed when making headline claims.

## 2. Actor-branch evidence boundary
Archived timing/intervention outputs for one DAD v4 line show effectively flat actor probabilities. Therefore, strong policy-level timing-control claims are not yet fully verified from those artifacts.

## 3. Ontology block maturity
The ontology construction pipeline, controlled evaluation manifest, and matched
DAD/A3D shared-launcher comparison block are now paper-facing and complete as
artifact evidence. The remaining issue is not missing ontology runs, but how
aggressively to generalize from a one-seed matched block without broader
multi-seed confirmation.

## 4. Seed scope
Multi-seed evidence currently exists for one DAD curriculum line and should be reported as a stability diagnostic with clearly limited scope.

## 5. External baseline comparability
Some published baselines are non-interpretable and use different backbones, objectives, or capacities. They should be presented honestly as context rather than as directly fairness-matched comparisons.

## 6. Intervention scope
Current intervention evidence supports partial structural intervenability, not large-scale causal control over the full actor policy.

## 7. Dataset scope
Main claims are currently strongest on DAD and A3D. Broader transfer across additional datasets remains future work.
